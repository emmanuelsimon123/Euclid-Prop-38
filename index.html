Euclid’s Elements — Book I, Proposition 38

Triangles on equal bases and in the same parallels are equal in area.

Tip: Hover or click a proof line to highlight the referenced figure. Use ☼ Focus to dim non‑relevant parts.

Guiding Question

If two triangles stand on equal bases and between the same parallels, how do we use parallelograms and earlier results to prove their areas are equal?

Need a hint?
  • I.36: Parallelograms on equal bases in the same parallels are equal (in area).
  • I.34: A diagonal bisects a parallelogram; each triangle cut off is half its parallelogram.
